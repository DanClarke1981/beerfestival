The brewery selection

The Titanic brewery are helping us organise the festival this year and we will be stocking a few of their ales too, for example;

  • Mild

    ABV 3.5%

    A mild of classic style full of roast malt and balanced by delicate hops. True to type it has a rounded sweetness and a smooth dry finish.

  • Lifeboat

    ABV 4.0%

    A mild of classic style full of roast malt and balanced by delicate hops. True to type it has a rounded sweetness and a smooth dry finish.

  • Stout

    ABV 4.5%

    A true old fashioned stout. A dark combination of malt and roast with some hops. Strongly flavoured and well balanced.

  • White Star

    ABV 4.8%

    A light refreshing distinctively hoppy beer with a freshness that belies its strength, so easy to drink it will fool all but the crustiest of sea dogs.

  • Anchor

    ABV 4.1%

    This impressive russet gold beer has a strong fruity aroma. Bitterness in the initial taste slips gently from the mooring to allow the full impact of the hops to burst through.

  • Iceberg

    ABV 4.1%

    A combination of Maris Otter pale malt and fine wheat malt, give this refreshing beer real zest. Add refreshing Yakima Galena and Cascade hops and what you get is a fantastic wheat beer that will hole any passing thirst.

  • Captain Smiths

    ABV 5.2%

    A red/Brown, full bodied beer hoppy and bitter with a sweetness and roast malt flavour and a good strong finish. Named after one of the most famous of sea captains of all time - Captain Edward Smith, who was born in Stoke on Trent.

  • Steerage

    ABV 3.5%

    Steerage passengers paid the least to travel aboard the Titanic but still found themselves surrounded by luxury they could never have dreamed of. This beer embodies the opulence that using only the finest ingredients brings, sit back relax and enjoy the trip that this refreshing clean drinking, hoppy golden bitter brings, then let the fruit, malt and predominantly hops carry through to the aftertaste.

Our 2012 Festival Ales

(You can expect a similar range in 2013)

Pavillion Pale Ale

ABV 4.5%. Banktop Brewery, Bolton. Created shortly after the brewery moved site to a tennis club pavilion, this pale ale has a big fruity flavour with a peppery hoppiness and a dry, bitter yet fruity finish.

Combine Harvest

ABV 4.4%. Batemans Brewery, Wainfleet, Lincolnshire. A unique multigrain beer, which is a combination of malted barley, oats wheat and rye, creating a light coloured refreshing beer with a dry/sweet character and hints of citrus.

Sunny Daze

ABV 3.6%. Big Lamp Brewery, Newcastle upon Tyne. This is a very light coloured beer, well hopped with Czechoslovakian styrian Goldings. Ideal for summer sessions.

BFG

ABV 4.2%. Black Country Brewery, Lower Gornal. A straw coloured quaffing beer, with an impressive bold citrus hop aroma, fruity balanced sweetness and a lingering refreshing aftertaste.

Fireside

ABV 5%. Black Country Brewery, Lower Gornal. A well rounded premium bitter,amber in colour, clean in taste, leading to a pleasant dry rewarding finish.

BG Sips

ABV 4%. Blue Monkey Brewery, Nottingham. A pale and intensely hoppy beer. Enticing tropical fruit aromas, finished with a good level of thirst quenching, almost peppery bitterness.

Damson Porter

ABV 4.5%. Burton Bridge Brewery, Burton on Trent. Fruity beer with a distinctive bitter-sweet after palate

Screech Owl

ABV 5.4%. Castle Rock Brewery, Nottingham. Originally one of the 12 ‘Natural Selection’ brews back in October 2008 to support the Nottinghamshire Wildlife Trust’s Adopt a Species Campaign, it immediately won the Society of Independent Brewers (Midlands) Supreme Champion Beer award at the Robin Hood Beer Festival and followed this success by winning the SIBA National Strong Bitter Title. Brewed to export strength this strong well hopped India Pale Ale has become a firm favourite with followers of the style.

Blueberry Ale

ABV 5%. Coach House Brewery, Warrington. A traditionally brewed beer but with the addition of fresh Blueberries and natural Blueberry flavouring. The beer is a wonderful pale golden colour with a light hoppy aroma and a distinctly fruity aftertaste. Currently SIBA National Gold MedalWinner 2011 – Voted Best Speciality Beer in the Country.

Golden Newt

ABV 4.1%. Elgoods Brewery, Wisbech. Pale in colour with prominent hoppy palate and full flavoured finish, Golden Newt is a pale session bitter of 4.1% ABV.

Pheasant Plucker

ABV 4.2%. Fuzzy Duck Brewery, Poulton le Fylde. Golden beer with a slightly spicy taste and a citrus finish from the late addition of Cascade hops

Cunning Stunt

ABV 4.3%. Fuzzy Duck Brewery, Poulton le Fylde. Golden coloured single-hop beer brewed with American Willamette hops. The rub of the hops gives an estery/blackcurrant/herbal aroma.

XB

ABV 4.1%. Holdens Brewery, Dudley. A pale golden brew, with slight sweetness balanced by Fuggles hop notes. There is a good hop aroma too. The finish is slightly malty

Edge

ABV 4.2%. Kinver Brewery, Kinver. Named after a local beauty spot and at 4.2% is a light hoppy bitter beer.

Khyber

ABV 5.8%. Kinver Brewery, Kinver. Strong and bitter 5.8% in true IPA fashion

Cwrw Gorslas

ABV 4%. Kite Brewery, Camarthen. ﻿An accomplished balance of spicy resinous hop aroma. The bitterness remains into the finish with dry fruit notes.

Stone Cutter

ABV 3.7%. Lymestone Brewery, Stone. From the very first draught, this pale hoppy ale will slake your thirst. A fusion of Maris Otter malts and Pilot and Fuggles hops explode on the palate as biscuit and juicy fruit flavours give way to citrus and berry hops, good hop aroma and hoppy after taste. A superb summer beer.

Stone Faced

ABV 4%. Lymestone Brewery, Stone. Pale and Dark Crystal Maris Otter malts unite to embrace a motherland of chewy toffee while fruits and berry flavours rush in. Styrian Goldings from the East and Nugget Hops from the West are allied in perfect harmony as pine and citrus hop flavours caress the taste buds.

Pendle Witches Brew

ABV 5.1%. Moorhouse Brewery, Burnley. A full malty flavoured beer with a crisp sweet fruity aftertaste and soft citrus nose

Outlandish Pale

ABV 3.9%. Offbeat Brewery, Crewe. It's zany, not your average session beer drinking above it's strength. The Outlandish Pale takes your palate to the extreme with a burst of lemony hoppiness.

Silver King

ABV 4.3%. Ossett, Ossett. This lager-style beer has a crisp, dry flavour and a fruity, citrus aroma.

TBA

ABV TBA%. Purity Brewery, Great Alne, Warks. TBA

Flintknapper

ABV 4.2%. Ramsbury Brewery, Marlborough, Wilts. A select blend of pale ale and chocolate malt – and our use of Goldings hops – have given this smooth ale a rich amber colour and a taste with malty overtones

Hop Twister

ABV 4.5%. Salopian Brewery, Shrewsbury. In 2012 has won silver in the Beer of Britain awards, and Gold in the CAMRA West Midlands Best Bitter awards. The palate is fresh, piercing and focused, with soft malt overtones and pronounced citrus flavours. A strong focus of taut, lemony, grapefruit on the finish which is balanced by a dry bitterness

Dark Ruby Mild

ABV 6%. Sarah Hughes Brewery, Sedgley. An award winning Dark Mild. A truly magnificent pint, it pours a lively dark cherry colour. Aroma hops are low, as you might expect, but this is about malt. Sweet, dark fruit cake taste with a bit of hop for balance. Hefty ABV for a session..although I have been known to try. Possibly too sweet for some, but a real old style mild of depth and complexity.

Shepherds Monument

ABV TBA%. Shugborough Brewery, Stafford. Brewed at Shugborough Hall in the traditional way

Mild

ABV 3.5%. Titanic Brewery, Stoke on Trent. A mild of classic style full of roast malt and balanced by delicate hops. True to type it has a rounded sweetness and a smooth dry finish

Iceberg

ABV 4.1%. Titanic Brewery, Stoke on Trent. A combination of Maris Otter pale malt and fine wheat malt, give this refreshing beer real zest. Add refreshing Yakima Galena and Cascade hops and what you get is a fantastic wheat beer that will hole any passing thirst

Triple Screw

ABV 4.5%. Titanic Brewery, Stoke on Trent. Deeply hoppy, stunningly malty and delightfully smooth,this three pronged attack on the taste buds is a treat for all beer lovers. Propel yourself to the bar for a Triple Screw.

Pier 54

ABV 5.4%. Titanic Brewery, Stoke on Trent. The intended berth for the Titanic when it arrived in New York, Pier 54 still stands today. This beer reflects the very best of US craft brewing, red in colour and with an inviting malty foretaste, the American hops come crashing through to wow those who love hops.

Plum Porter

ABV 4.9%. Titanic Brewery, Stoke on Trent. This beer is dark strong and well rounded; the richness of such a rotund beer is brought to an even keel by the late addition of Goldings hops and natural plum flavouring. Take the opportunity and go for the low hanging fruit, this sumptuous beer really is a plum!

Gold Bullion

ABV 3.8%. TSA Brewery, Stirling. Light golden beer with a nose of fresh peach and lime silky smooth with a sweet citrus and hoppy after taste.

Jury

ABV 4.6%. Draymans, Penkridge. A dark straw pale ale with a good balance of malt and fruityhop on the nose and palate. An initial sweetness followed by anultimately long bitter finish

Sunchaser

ABV 4.0%. Everards, Leicestershire . A thirst-quenching beer made in the style of a continental lager. Hence you will find subtle fruit flavours and a little sweetness within.

Quaff

ABV 3.7%%. Woods, Shropshire. A pale and refreshing light bitter with a clean, hoppy finish.

Our 2012 Festival Ciders

Black Rat Cider

ABV 6%. Moles Brewery, Melksham. A still, medium cider. Slightly cloudy and full flavoured. A traditional cider, full apple flavours, with a pleasant bite and finish. Fermented from straight apple juice with wild yeasts, this is a proper cider.

Devon Scumpy

ABV 6%. Sandford Devon Scrumpy, Crediton, Devon. CAMRA champion cider of Great Britain 2010, A still, medium cider. Slightly cloudy and full flavoured. A traditional cider, full apple flavours, with a pleasant bite and finish. Fermented straight from fresh apple juice with wild yeasts this is proper cider.

Perry

ABV 4.5%. Westons, Much Marcle, Herefordshire. Made from perry pears and fullly matured in old oak vats to develop its strength and traditional character. Free from all artificial colouring, flavouring and sweetening it retains its natural pale colour. English perry made in Herefordshire. Fermented and fully matured in old oak vats to develop an exquisite, delicate, light and fruity character. Softly floral with a clear natural pale straw colour.

Medium

ABV 6%. Burrow Hill, Martock, Somerset. Medium dry Somerset draught cider. Hazy yellow brown colour. A really good, fruity medium cider. perfect level of sweetness, balanced really well with a perfect level of dryness. Edging more towards dry than sweet. Epic apples. About as close to biting into an apple as any cider I’ve ever had. Excellent drying finish. Brilliant stuff.

Tumbledown

ABV 5.2%. Snail Bank Tumbledown, Bishops Frome, Worcs. Medium Cider. Hazy amber color, flat. Light apple aroma. The mouthfeel is dry and fairly acidic; it has more vinegar character than apple; the final is very dry. A rough cider, with some interest, but not very skilled.

Kingston Black

ABV 7.5%. Gwatkins, Hereford. The Kingston Black apple is the most famous apple in the cider making world. Dating back centuries. Gold with no head. The aroma is great, rich sweet apple, really fresh. The body a little less so, its fairly thin despite the ABV, the apple flavour is good and clean though, dry abd fruity.
