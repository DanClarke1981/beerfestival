2015 Festival Ales & Ciders

Ales

» Lifeboat (Medium) - 4.0%

Titanic Brewery (Stoke-on-Trent)

Cast yourself adrift with this fruity and malty,red brown bittersweet beer. It has a serene caramel character and the finish is dry and fruity.

» White Star (Light) - 4.5%

Titanic Brewery (Stoke-on-Trent)

This multi award winning beer has a light refreshing distinctively hoppy beer with a freshness that belies its strength, so easy to drink it will fool all but the crustiest of sea dogs.

» Barnsley Gold (Light) - 4.3%

Acorn Brewery (Barnsley)

This award winning golden beer has good bitterness levels, with a fresh citrus and hop aroma. A hoppy flavour throughout with a well hopped clean, dry finish. A CAMRA Champion beer of Britain.

» Poets Tipple (Medium) - 4.0%

Ashover Brewery (Chesterfield)

Chestnut to amber balanced malt beer. Triple hopped with English Challenger hops for a traditional bitter flavour.

» The Yard (Light) - 3.9%

Backyard Brewhouse (Brownhills)

A golden straw coloured beer brewed specially to mark the discovery of the 'Staffordshire Hoard'. SIBA National beer competition 2011 Gold medal.

» Batemans Gold (Light) - 3.9%

Batemans Brewery (Skegness)

A golden colour, refreshing beer brewed with lager malt and Chinook and Cascade hops, giving a lovely citrus flavour and aroma, which is quite dry and very moreish.

» Chase Buster (Light) - 4.5%

Beowolf Brewery (Chasewater)

A golden ale, caramel biscuit aroma, mild hop undertones of citrus fruits, well balanced, pleasant; an easy drinking ale.

» Top Dog Stout (Dark) - 5.0%

Burton Bridge Brewery (Burton-on-Trent)

Malty with full-bodied rounded bitterness, The Top Dog is Watchman III (a Staffordshire Bull Terrier) mascot of the Staffordshire Regiment, once a regular at the brewery tap.

» Black Gold (Dark) - 3.8%

Castle Rock (Nottingham)

A dark mild ale with a slight bitterness but without undue sweetness. A recipe that appeals to both established mild drinkers and also the younger palate.

» Salvation (Light) - 3.8%

Charnword Brewery (Loughborough)

A light refreshing golden beer, with tropical fruit, citrus, and floral flavours. American Cascade and Amarillo hops create a citrus aroma, and crisp clean bitterness on the finish.

» Honeypot (Medium) - 3.8%

Coach House Brewing Company (Warrington)

Brewed with pure Cheshire honey. The aroma has notes of malt and caramel. The flavour is sweet with notes of malt and caramel, leading to a dry toffee finish.

» Commando Hoofing (Medium) - 4.0%

Cotleigh Brewery (Somerset)

A pale golden beer, refreshing and slightly sparkling. Originally brewed to celebrate the return of the Royal Marines 40 Commando from deployment in Afghanistan and to further support the unit that is based in Taunton, Commando Hoofing beer now enjoys widespread acclaim. When a number of Royal Marines from Termoli Troop 40 Commando RM tasted the beer, they said "It's a great beer with an explosion of flavours from hops to fresh fruit and it's far safer and far more palatable than ones we are used to". Hoofing' is Royal Marine slang to describe something fantastic e.g. a hoofing dancer. This beer is certainly that ... Hoofing!

» 22 (Medium) - 4.3%

Dancing Duck Brewery (Derby)

A wonderfully balanced best bitter with good malty flavour and dark fruit notes, these are offset by a strong hop with a very clean finish. A great thirst quenching beer for any time of the day.

» Winter Meltdown (Medium) - 5.0%

Dark Star Brewing Company (West Sussex)

Brewed with Chocolate and Crystal malts to produce this deep bronze coloured beer with a hint of malty flavour, bittered by the traditional Golding hop varieties, this is a classic strong bitter complimented by the aromatic warmth of Chinese stem ginger.

» Double Mash (Medium) - 4.6%

Derby Brewing Company (Derby)

A classic mid-brown coloured strong bitter, with a markedly bitter finish from the generous use of Styrian hops. Flavoursome English pale malt adds balance and a smooth finish, which lasts and lasts. Full-bodied, yet with an ease of drinking which belies its strength.

» Chocolate Cherry Mild (Dark) - 3.8%

Dunham Massey Brewing Company (Altrincham)

Chocolate Cherry is a multi-award winning speciality mild. Champion Speciality Beer of Britain 2012. It has all the class of Dunham Dark, with a dry hint of cherry that cuts through the malt flavours.

» Dunscar Blonde (Light) - 3.8%

Dunscar Bridge Brewery (Blackburn)

A refreshing Blonde session ale made with US and European hops, for a beer with a distinctive hoppy bite.

» Sunchaser (Light) - 4.0%

Everards Brewery (Leceister)

Sunchaser Blonde is a thirst-quenching beer made in the style of a continental lager. Hence you will find subtle fruit flavours and a little sweetness within.

» Marmalade Cat (Medium) - 5.5%

Fat Cat Brewery (Norwich)

Our flavoursome copper ale combines English pale malt with speciality Munich malt for complexity and a smooth finish. Only traditional English hops are used and the result is a multi-award winner, once nominated for Champion Beer of Britain.

» The Don Jon (Light) - 4.8%

Grafton Brewery (Leceister)

A super pale ale brewed with lager malt. This beer is moderately hopped and leaves a pleasant taste.

» Black Country Bitter (Medium) - 3.8%

Holdens Brewery (Dudley)

A Dark amber premium beer with robust malty overtones and a bitter sweet full bodied taste. Brewed using the Finest English Maris Otter Malt and Goldings hops this big, beautiful malty monster is an industrial strength beer at it's best.

» Taiphoon (Light) - 4.2%

Hop Back Brewery (Salisbury)

A fantastic pale gold beer with hints of Lemongrass and coriander.

» Kelham Best (Medium) - 3.8%

Kelham Island Brewery (Sheffield)

A golden ale brewed with a blend of pale malts with a hint of crystal malt and roasted barley. The hops are British fuggles and goldings supplemented with hops from the other side of the Atlantic. A sweet aroma is of incense and elderberry with hints of caramel spicyness.

» Half Centurian (Light) - 5.0%

Kinver Brewery (Kinver)

Midlands Beer of the Year Gold Champion - 2009 SIBA Premium Bitter Category. Brewed to celebrate the 50th birthdays of both founders. A 5.0% pale premium bitter with hop and citrus flavours.

» Little John (Medium) - 4.3%

Lincoln Green Brewing Company (Nottingham)

A smooth, dark amber malty ale with balanced bitterness.

» Bier Head (Medium) - 4.1%

Liverpool Organic Brewery (Liverpool)

Best bitter with hoppy foretaste complex spice and crisp malt tones.

» Angry Bird (Medium) - 4.0%

Magpie Brewery (Nottingham)

Ruby red hoppy ale. Malt character gives way to a rich hoppy palate. 2p per pint is donated to the Ear Foundation.

» Oktoberfest (Dark) - 4.2%

Milestone Brewery (Newark)

A limited edition seasonal beer - deep bronze in colour.

» Dreadnought (Medium) - 4.8%

Nottingham Brewery (Nottingham)

A rich ruby coloured premium bitter with a well rounded fullness of malt and deep strength of hop bitterness.

» Sooty Oatmeal Stout (Dark) - 4.8%

Nottingham Brewery (Nottingham)

A traditional classic oatmeal stout brewed to an original recipe using only the finest barley malt and choicest hops. "It's food and drink in a glass". Only available during the winter season. It was Champion Beer of the 2002 Cambridge CAMRA Beer Festival.

» Scarlet Macaw (Medium) - 4.4%

Oakham Ales (Peterborough)

Tart gooseberry and soft peach on the nose. Gooseberries and fruit to taste, before an intense bitterness that's as sharp as a macaw's screech!

» Disfunctional IPA (Light) - 4.8%

Offbeat Brewery (Crewe)

A big hoppy fruity IPA.

» Gold Rush (Light) - 4.5%

Prospect Brewery (Wigan)

Deep golden ale with a fresh sharp smell and a distintive hoppy and bitter taste. Named after the infamous Klondike Goldrush of 1896.

» Butlers Revenge (Light) - 4.1%

Shugborough Brewery (Milford)

In the great houses the butler was responsible for the brewing of fine ales for all the household. Weak "Boy's Bitter" for the labourers and strong ales for the masters. The finest of them all however he would keep for himself! This pale powerful ale is straight from his secret stash and whilst the colour and delightful hoppy character suggest easy drinking the smooth finish will leave your palette refreshed. Let the Master wait.

» Centurians Ghost (Dark) - 5.4%

York Brewery (York)

Centurion's Ghost is a multi gold medal award winning dark ruby ale. It has a smooth roasted malt palate, and a combination of Challenger, Fuggle and Bramling Cross hops give it a subtle bitterness with autumn fruit flavours that linger on the aftertaste.

» Guzzler (Light) - 3.6%

York Brewery (York)

A beautifully balanced, light gold coloured beer with dominant hop flavours and pale malt character. Guzzler is an easy drinking, crisp & refreshing session ale brewed with Challenger and Celeia hops that bring about a light citrus palate with overtones of Grapefruit.

Ciders

» Explorer (Medium) - 4.5%

Orchard Pig (Somerset)

The bittersweet aroma and taste of cider country with a lingering ripe fruit sweetness - easy drinking Somerset cider.

» The Hogfather (Strong) - 7.4%

Orchard Pig (Somerset)

Well here's the Pig's Daddy...at 7.4% not for the faint hearted - close your eyes and savour the Somerset cider apples in this one. Don't be fooled by the impressive muscle power of this cider, you can still expect a smooth, drinkably moreish finish.

» Cheddar Valley (Strong) - 6.0%

Thatchers Cider (Somerset)

The colour of this unique cider gives it a vibrant character all of its own for those who want an alternative refreshing drink with a touch of natural sparkle.Naturally fermented, Cheddar valley is matured in oak vats to ensure the distinctive full flavour is retained.

» Heritage (Medium) - 4.9%

Thatchers Cider (Somerset)

Heritage is a popular, traditional cider - it really looks and tastes like a 'proper cider' but you'll find its light touch and balanced flavours offer a drink that appeals to all comers. This really is a drink that reflects Thatchers expertise in crafting fine cider.

» Family Reserve (Medium) - 5.0%

Westons Cider (Herefordshire)

A clear bright still cider with a smooth, well-balanced fruity flavour and clean apple finish. Suitable for vegetarians, vegans and coeliacs.

» Westons Country Perry (Perry) - 4.6%

Westons Cider (Herefordshire)

Made from perry pears and fullly matured in old oak vats to develop its strength and traditional character. Free from all artificial colouring, flavouring and sweetening it retains its natural pale colour. Suitable for vegetarians, vegans and coeliacs.

Lagers

» Liberty Pils - 4.4%

Freedom Brewery (Blythe)

Our redesigned pilsner, brewed at the classic abv and with the perfect balanced hop profile that is truly characteristic of this style. Balanced hop flavours, very subtle malt, complex hop finish.

» Wai - Kiwa - 4.5%

Freedom Brewery (Blythe)

A mix of New Zealand and American hops (Wakatu and Calypso). Crisp, clean and dry with vanilla and lime zest.
